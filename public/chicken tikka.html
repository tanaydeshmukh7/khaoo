Chicken Tikka

The timeless tandoori classic ‚Äî succulent chunks of chicken marinated in yogurt and spices, grilled to perfection with smoky, charred edges.

Ingredients

For the First Marinade:

  • 500g boneless chicken (cut into 2-inch cubes)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Second Marinade:

  • 1 cup thick yogurt (hung curd preferred)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ¬Ω tsp black pepper powder
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp gram flour (besan), roasted
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • Red food color (optional)
  • Salt to taste

For Grilling:

  • 1 large onion, cut into squares
  • 1 large bell pepper (capsicum), cut into squares
  • 2 tbsp melted butter or ghee (for basting)
  • Wooden or metal skewers

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Lemon wedges
  • Onion rings
  • Chaat masala for sprinkling

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. First Marinade:
    • Wash and pat dry the chicken pieces.
    • Cut into uniform 2-inch cubes for even cooking.
    • Mix chicken with lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt.
    • Let it sit for 15 minutes. This helps tenderize the meat.
  2. Prepare Second Marinade:
    • In a large bowl, whisk thick yogurt until smooth.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste, kasuri methi (crush between palms), all spice powders, roasted gram flour, oil, and salt.
    • If using, add a pinch of red food color for vibrant tandoori color.
    • Mix everything well to form a thick, smooth marinade.
  3. Marinate the Chicken:
    • Add the first-marinated chicken to the yogurt mixture.
    • Coat each piece thoroughly with the marinade.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight for best results).
    • The longer it marinates, the more flavorful and tender it becomes.
  4. Prepare for Grilling:
    • If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning.
    • Thread marinated chicken pieces onto skewers.
    • Alternate with onion and bell pepper squares for added flavor.
    • Leave some space between pieces for even cooking.
  5. Grill the Tikka (Choose Your Method):
    • Oven Method: Preheat oven to 220¬∞C (425¬∞F). Place skewers on a wire rack over a baking tray. Grill for 15-20 minutes, turning halfway and basting with butter.
    • Stovetop Method: Heat a grill pan or tawa on high. Cook skewers for 12-15 minutes, turning frequently and basting with butter.
    • Tandoor/BBQ: Grill over high heat for 10-12 minutes, turning and basting regularly until charred edges appear.
    • The chicken should have beautiful char marks and be cooked through.
  6. Add Smoky Flavor (Optional):
    • Place grilled tikkas in a bowl.
    • Make a small foil cup and place a piece of hot charcoal in it.
    • Place the cup in the bowl with chicken.
    • Pour ¬Ω tsp ghee over the charcoal and immediately cover the bowl.
    • Let it smoke for 2-3 minutes, then remove the charcoal.
  7. Serve:
    • Arrange tikka pieces on a serving platter.
    • Sprinkle chaat masala and chopped coriander.
    • Serve hot with mint chutney, onion rings, and lemon wedges.
    • Perfect as an appetizer or main course with naan and salad!

Variations & Tips üçΩÔ∏è

  • Use hung curd (strained yogurt) for a thicker marinade that sticks better to the chicken.
  • Roast the gram flour (besan) before adding to remove raw taste and add nuttiness.
  • Mustard oil gives authentic tandoori flavor, but any oil works fine.
  • For extra tenderness, add 1 tsp raw papaya paste or meat tenderizer to the marinade.
  • Don't skip the double marinade ‚Äî the first one tenderizes, the second adds flavor.
  • Basting with butter or ghee during grilling is essential for juiciness and flavor.
  • You can make Paneer Tikka using the same marinade with paneer cubes.
  • Leftover tikka makes amazing Chicken Tikka Masala ‚Äî just add it to a tomato-cream gravy!

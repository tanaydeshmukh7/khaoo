Paneer Tadka

Ingredients

For the Paneer:

  • 400g paneer (cut into 1-inch cubes)
  • 1 tbsp oil (for light frying)
  • Warm water for soaking

For the Gravy:

  • 3 large tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 2 large onions, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods

Spice Powders:

  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste

For the Tadka (Tempering):

  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • A pinch of hing (asafoetida)
  • ¬Ω tsp kasuri methi

Other Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • ¬Ω cup fresh cream or malai
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp sugar (to balance acidity)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • ¬Ω cup water (adjust for consistency)

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Paneer:
    • Cut paneer into 1-inch cubes.
    • Soak in warm water for 10 minutes to keep it soft.
    • Optionally, lightly fry in 1 tbsp oil until golden (this adds texture).
    • Set aside the paneer cubes.
  2. Make Tomato-Onion Puree:
    • Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and onions.
    • Cook for 5-7 minutes until soft and mushy.
    • Let it cool, then blend into a smooth puree.
    • Strain if you want a silky smooth gravy.
  3. Prepare the Base:
    • Heat 3 tbsp oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom.
    • Saut√© for 30 seconds until aromatic.
  4. Cook the Aromatics:
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies.
    • Saut√© for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt.
    • Mix well and cook for 30 seconds.
  5. Add the Puree and Simmer:
    • Pour in the tomato-onion puree.
    • Mix well and cook on medium heat for 8-10 minutes.
    • Cook until oil starts separating from the sides.
    • Add sugar to balance the tanginess.
    • Add water to achieve desired gravy consistency.
  6. Add Paneer and Cream:
    • Gently add the paneer cubes to the gravy.
    • Mix carefully to coat paneer with gravy.
    • Add fresh cream and butter, mix gently.
    • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add.
    • Sprinkle garam masala on top.
    • Simmer for 3-4 minutes on low heat.
  7. Prepare the Tadka:
    • In a small tadka pan, heat 2 tbsp ghee or butter until smoking hot.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
    • Add chopped garlic and dried red chilies.
    • Add a pinch of hing and kasuri methi.
    • Saut√© for 10-15 seconds until garlic turns golden.
    • Immediately pour this sizzling tadka over the paneer gravy.
  8. Serve:
    • Give it a gentle stir after adding the tadka.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Drizzle a little cream on top for presentation.
    • Serve piping hot with naan, roti, paratha, or jeera rice!

Variations & Tips üßà

  • For softer paneer, always soak cubes in warm water before adding to gravy.
  • Frying paneer is optional ‚Äî skip it for a healthier version.
  • Strain the tomato-onion puree for restaurant-style smooth gravy.
  • The tadka is what makes this dish special ‚Äî don't skip it!
  • Use fresh cream or malai for authentic taste; avoid packaged cream if possible.
  • Kasuri methi adds incredible depth of flavor ‚Äî crush it well between palms.
  • Add 1 tsp honey instead of sugar for a subtle sweetness.
  • For spicier version, add more green chilies or red chili powder.
  • You can add bell peppers and onion chunks for Paneer Tadka Masala style.
  • Add a few cashew nuts to the puree for extra richness.
  • Store in refrigerator for up to 2 days; tastes even better the next day!

Dal Tadka

Ingredients

For the Dal:

  • 1 cup toor dal (yellow pigeon peas)
  • ¬º cup masoor dal (red lentils) - optional for creamier texture
  • 3 cups water
  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp oil or ghee

For the Masala Base:

  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • ¬Ω tsp red chili powder
  • ¬Ω tsp coriander powder
  • ¬º tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee

For the Tadka (Tempering):

  • 3 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ¬Ω tsp mustard seeds
  • A pinch of hing (asafoetida)
  • 6-8 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2-3 dried red chilies, broken
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh cream (optional)
  • Lemon wedges
  • Ginger julienne

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Wash and Soak the Dal:
    • Rinse toor dal and masoor dal together 2-3 times until water runs clear.
    • Soak in water for 20-30 minutes (optional but reduces cooking time).
    • Drain the water before cooking.
  2. Cook the Dal (Choose Your Method):
    • Pressure Cooker Method: Add dal, 3 cups water, turmeric, salt, and 1 tsp oil. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles on medium heat. Let pressure release naturally.
    • Stovetop Method: In a pot, add dal, 4 cups water, turmeric, and salt. Bring to boil, then simmer for 30-40 minutes until dal is soft and mushy. Stir occasionally.
    • Instant Pot Method: Add dal, water, turmeric, and salt. Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes, then natural release for 10 minutes.
    • Mash the dal slightly with a spoon for creamy consistency.
  3. Prepare the Masala Base:
    • Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a pan.
    • Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cook for 1-2 minutes.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy (5-7 minutes).
    • The tomatoes should break down completely.
  4. Add Spices:
    • Add red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt to the tomato-onion mixture.
    • Cook the spices for 1-2 minutes until oil separates.
    • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add.
    • Mix well and cook for another minute.
  5. Combine Dal with Masala:
    • Add the cooked dal to the masala mixture.
    • Mix everything well and bring to a boil.
    • Add water if needed to adjust consistency (it should be slightly thin).
    • Simmer for 5-7 minutes on low heat for flavors to blend.
    • Add garam masala and mix.
  6. Prepare the Tadka (The Star Step!):
    • Heat 3 tbsp ghee in a small tadka pan until smoking hot.
    • Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds, let them crackle.
    • Add a pinch of hing (asafoetida).
    • Add chopped garlic and saut√© until golden brown.
    • Add dried red chilies and curry leaves (be careful, they splutter!).
    • Crush kasuri methi and add to the tadka.
    • The tadka should be aromatic and sizzling.
  7. Finish and Serve:
    • Pour the hot, sizzling tadka over the dal.
    • You'll hear the amazing sizzle ‚Äî that's the magic moment!
    • Mix gently or serve with tadka on top for presentation.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander, ginger julienne, and a swirl of cream.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice, roti, naan, or jeera rice!
    • Don't forget lemon wedges on the side for that tangy kick!

Variations & Tips ü•£

  • For creamier dal, add ¬º cup masoor dal or moong dal to the toor dal.
  • The tadka is what makes this dish special ‚Äî use generous amounts of ghee!
  • Don't skip the garlic in tadka ‚Äî it adds incredible flavor.
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek) is essential for authentic dhaba-style taste.
  • The dal should be slightly thin as it thickens upon cooling.
  • For restaurant-style dal, add 1-2 tbsp butter at the end.
  • Add a squeeze of lemon juice just before serving for brightness.
  • For Dal Fry variation, reduce water for thicker consistency.
  • For Tadka Dal, the tadka is poured on top; for Dal Tadka, tadka is mixed in.
  • You can make this in bulk ‚Äî it tastes even better the next day!
  • Add a pinch of sugar to balance the tanginess of tomatoes.
  • Store in refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze for up to a month.

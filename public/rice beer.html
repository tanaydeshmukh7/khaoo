Rice Beer (Apong/Xaj)

Important Note: This recipe is for traditional rice beer preparation. The brewing process involves natural fermentation. Please ensure you are of legal drinking age in your region. Always consume responsibly and follow local laws regarding home brewing.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 2 kg sticky rice (glutinous rice) or regular rice
  • 50-100 grams rice yeast cake (marcha/bakhar) - available at Asian stores
  • Water for cooking and fermenting

Equipment Needed:

  • Large pot for cooking rice
  • Clean earthen pot or food-grade plastic container with lid
  • Clean cloth or muslin
  • Banana leaves (optional, traditional)

Step-by-Step Brewing Process

  1. Cook the Rice:
    • Wash the rice thoroughly 2-3 times and drain.
    • Cook the rice until it's fully cooked but not mushy (slightly firm).
    • You can steam or boil the rice - steaming is preferred for traditional rice beer.
    • Spread the cooked rice on a clean cloth or tray to cool completely to room temperature.
    • The rice should be completely cool before adding yeast.
  2. Prepare the Yeast:
    • Take the rice yeast cake (marcha/bakhar) and crush it into a fine powder.
    • If the yeast cake is hard, you can use a mortar and pestle or grinder.
    • Sift to remove any large particles.
  3. Mix Rice with Yeast:
    • Once the rice is completely cool, transfer it to a large clean bowl.
    • Sprinkle the powdered yeast evenly over the rice.
    • Mix gently but thoroughly with clean hands, ensuring the yeast is evenly distributed.
    • Add a few tablespoons of lukewarm water if the mixture seems too dry.
    • The rice should be slightly moist but not wet.
  4. Fermentation Process:
    • Transfer the yeast-mixed rice into a clean earthen pot or food-grade container.
    • If using banana leaves, line the pot with them (traditional method).
    • Press the rice mixture gently and flatten the top.
    • Make a small depression or hole in the center (this is where the liquid will collect).
    • Cover with a clean cloth and then with a lid.
    • Store in a warm, dark place for fermentation.
    • The ideal temperature is 25-30¬∞C (77-86¬∞F).
  5. Monitor Fermentation:
    • After 24-48 hours, you'll notice liquid collecting in the center depression.
    • The rice will start to smell sweet and slightly alcoholic.
    • Let it ferment for 3-7 days depending on desired strength and taste.
    • Shorter fermentation = milder, sweeter taste.
    • Longer fermentation = stronger, more alcoholic taste.
  6. Extract and Serve:
    • After fermentation, the rice beer is ready.
    • To drink, add water to the fermented rice (about 2-3 cups per cup of rice).
    • Mix well and let it sit for 10-15 minutes.
    • Strain through a clean cloth or fine sieve.
    • The liquid is your rice beer!
    • Serve chilled or at room temperature.
    • The leftover rice can be used for another extraction by adding more water.

Tips & Variations üç∫

  • Cleanliness is crucial - all utensils and containers must be thoroughly clean to prevent bad bacteria.
  • Temperature control is key - too cold and fermentation won't happen, too hot and it may spoil.
  • The yeast cake (marcha) is essential - it contains wild yeasts and enzymes for fermentation.
  • Sticky rice gives a sweeter, more traditional taste, but regular rice works too.
  • The first extraction is the strongest, subsequent extractions are milder.
  • Store fermented rice in the refrigerator to slow down fermentation after desired taste is achieved.
  • Different tribes have variations - some add herbs, some use different rice varieties.
  • In Sikkim, it's called Chhaang and often served warm in bamboo containers.
  • Rice beer is rich in probiotics and B vitamins.
  • Always taste test during fermentation to achieve your preferred flavor profile.
  • Consume within 2-3 days after extraction for best taste.

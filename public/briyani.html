Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 green cardamoms
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish & Flavor:

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced & fried till golden
  • Handful of fresh mint leaves
  • Handful of fresh coriander leaves
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  • 2 tbsp ghee or clarified butter

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Chicken Marinade:
    • Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice.
    • Coat the chicken pieces with the marinade and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is better).
  2. Cook the Rice:
    • Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak for 30 minutes.
    • Boil water with bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and salt.
    • Add the soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry Onions:
    • Heat oil in a pan and fry thinly sliced onions till golden brown and crispy.
    • Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Layer the Biryani:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread half the cooked rice.
    • Place marinated chicken pieces on top of the rice.
    • Add the remaining rice as the top layer.
    • Sprinkle saffron milk, fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves.
  5. Dum Cooking (Slow Steam):
    • Seal the pot with dough or tight-fitting lid to trap steam.
    • Cook on very low flame for 25‚Äì30 minutes.
    • Alternatively, place a skillet under the pot for heat insulation.
  6. Serve:
    • Gently mix the biryani layers before serving to distribute chicken and rice evenly.
    • Serve hot with raita, mirchi ka salan, or simple yogurt.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes (or any starchy potato)
  • Oil for deep frying (vegetable, canola, or peanut oil)
  • Salt to taste

Optional Seasonings:

  • Black pepper powder
  • Garlic powder
  • Paprika or chili powder
  • Dried herbs (oregano, thyme, or rosemary)
  • Parmesan cheese (for loaded fries)

For Serving:

  • Ketchup
  • Mayonnaise
  • Cheese sauce
  • Ranch dressing
  • BBQ sauce

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Potatoes:
    • Peel the potatoes (optional - you can keep the skin for rustic fries).
    • Cut potatoes into even sticks about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.
    • Place cut potatoes in a large bowl of cold water and let them soak for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours).
    • This removes excess starch and helps achieve crispier fries.
  2. Dry the Potatoes:
    • Drain the water completely.
    • Pat the potato sticks thoroughly dry with paper towels or a clean kitchen towel.
    • Make sure they are completely dry before frying to avoid oil splattering.
  3. First Fry (Blanching):
    • Heat oil in a deep pot or fryer to 325¬∞F (160¬∞C).
    • Carefully add potatoes in small batches, don't overcrowd the pot.
    • Fry for 5-7 minutes until potatoes are cooked through but still pale (not golden).
    • Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
    • Let them cool for at least 10 minutes (or refrigerate for up to 2 hours).
  4. Second Fry (Crisping):
    • Heat the oil to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C) for the second fry.
    • Add the blanched fries back in small batches.
    • Fry for 3-5 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove and drain on fresh paper towels.
    • Immediately season with salt while they're still hot.
    • Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce!

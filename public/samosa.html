Prep Time

Cook Time

30 mins

Serves

6 People

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water (as needed for kneading)

For the Filling:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1 tsp ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

For Frying:

  • Oil for deep frying

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and carom seeds.
    • Add oil or ghee and rub it into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Gradually add water and knead into a firm, smooth dough.
    • Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 20-30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds and fennel seeds.
    • When they crackle, add ginger and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
    • Add boiled peas and mash them slightly.
    • Add coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, amchur, and salt.
    • Mix in mashed potatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes.
    • Add chopped coriander leaves, mix well, and let the filling cool completely.
  3. Shape the Samosas:
    • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll into balls.
    • Roll each ball into an oval shape, then cut it in half to make two semicircles.
    • Take one semicircle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with water.
    • Fill the cone with 1-2 tablespoons of the potato filling.
    • Seal the open edge with water, pressing firmly to close. You can crimp the edges for a decorative look.
    • Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
  4. Fry the Samosas:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat.
    • Gently slide in 3-4 samosas at a time (don't overcrowd).
    • Fry on low to medium heat until golden brown and crispy (about 12-15 minutes).
    • Turn occasionally for even cooking.
    • Remove and drain on paper towels.
    • Serve hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or tomato ketchup!

  • For extra crispy samosas, add a tablespoon of rice flour to the dough.
  • Always fry on low to medium heat to ensure the samosas cook through without burning.
  • Make sure the filling is completely cool before stuffing to prevent the dough from becoming soggy.
  • Seal the edges properly with water to prevent them from opening while frying.
  • You can make samosas ahead and freeze them unfried. Fry directly from frozen when needed.
  • Try variations with paneer, chicken, or lamb keema filling!
  • Serve with masala chai for the ultimate Indian snack experience!

Dhokla

Prep Time

10 mins

4 People

Difficulty

Easy

Ingredients

For the Batter:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (rava/sooji)
  • 3/4 cup water (or buttermilk for tangier taste)
  • 1 tsp ginger-green chili paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp fruit salt (Eno) or baking soda

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (til)
  • 6-8 curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tbsp water

For Garnish:

  • 2 tbsp freshly grated coconut
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

For Serving:

  • Green chutney
  • Tamarind chutney

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Steamer:
    • Grease a dhokla plate or any flat-bottomed dish with oil.
    • Fill a steamer or pressure cooker with water (about 2 inches) and bring it to a boil.
    • If using a pressure cooker, remove the whistle and rubber gasket.
  2. Make the Batter:
    • In a large bowl, mix gram flour, semolina, turmeric powder, sugar, and salt.
    • Add ginger-green chili paste.
    • Gradually add water (or buttermilk) and whisk to make a smooth, lump-free batter.
    • The batter should be of pouring consistency - not too thick or thin.
    • Let it rest for 5 minutes.
    • Add lemon juice and mix well.
  3. Add Fruit Salt and Steam:
    • Just before steaming, add fruit salt (Eno) to the batter.
    • Add 1-2 teaspoons of water over the fruit salt and mix gently in one direction.
    • The batter will become frothy and airy - don't overmix.
    • Immediately pour the batter into the greased plate.
    • Place the plate in the steamer and steam on medium-high heat for 12-15 minutes.
    • Check with a toothpick - it should come out clean when done.
    • Turn off heat and let it rest for 2-3 minutes before removing.
  4. Prepare Tempering and Serve:
    • Once dhokla cools slightly, cut into square or diamond shapes.
    • Heat oil in a small pan for tempering.
    • Add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, and asafoetida.
    • When mustard seeds crackle, add 2 tablespoons of water (be careful, it will splutter).
    • Pour this tempering evenly over the dhokla pieces.
    • Garnish with grated coconut and chopped coriander leaves.
    • Serve warm with green chutney and tamarind chutney!

Tips & Variations üåø

  • The key to soft dhokla is not overmixing after adding fruit salt - mix gently and steam immediately.
  • For tangier dhokla, use buttermilk or curd instead of water in the batter.
  • Make sure the steamer water is boiling before placing the dhokla plate inside.
  • Don't steam for too long or dhokla will become hard.
  • For traditional fermented dhokla: Soak and grind rice and urad dal, ferment overnight, then steam.
  • Try rava dhokla: Use only semolina instead of besan for a different texture.
  • Try sandwich dhokla: Make two layers with green chutney in between.
  • Add veggies: Mix in grated carrots or finely chopped spinach to the batter.
  • Leftover dhokla can be cut into cubes and used to make dhokla chaat!
  • Best enjoyed fresh and warm - pairs perfectly with masala chai!

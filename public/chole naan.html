Chole Naan

Spicy, tangy chickpea curry (chole) served with soft, fluffy naan bread. A hearty North Indian street food favorite that's perfect for any meal!

Start Cooking

Prep Time

20 mins (+ overnight soaking)

Cook Time

60 mins

Serves

4 People

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

For the Chole:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (kabuli chana), soaked overnight
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 black tea bag (or 1 tsp black tea leaves in muslin cloth, for color)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

For the Chole Masala:

  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ¬Ω tsp chole masala (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 onion, sliced into rings (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges for serving

For the Naan:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ¬Ω cup warm milk
  • ¬º cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp melted butter or ghee
  • ¬Ω tsp salt
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds (kalonji) or sesame seeds (optional)
  • Water as needed for dough
  • Butter for brushing

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas:
    • Soak the dried chickpeas in water overnight (or at least 8 hours). They should double in size.
    • Drain and rinse the chickpeas. Pressure cook with 3 cups water, ¬Ω tsp salt, and the tea bag (or tea leaves in cloth) for 3-4 whistles until soft but not mushy.
    • Let pressure release naturally. Discard the tea bag. Reserve the cooking water (aquafaba) for gravy.
    • If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse, then simmer in water with tea for 10-15 mins for color.
  2. Make the Chole Masala Base:
    • Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom. Saut√© for 30 seconds until fragrant.
    • Add chopped onions and green chilies. Cook until onions turn golden brown (8-10 minutes).
    • Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until raw smell disappears.
  3. Cook the Gravy:
    • Add tomato puree, red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt.
    • Cook on medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring until the masala thickens and oil separates.
    • Add the cooked chickpeas along with 1-1¬Ω cups of reserved cooking water (adjust for desired consistency).
    • Bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 15-20 minutes to let flavors meld.
    • Add garam masala, amchur (or lemon juice), and chole masala if using. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
    • Mash a few chickpeas lightly for thicker gravy. Taste and adjust salt/spices.
  4. Prepare the Naan Dough:
    • In a small bowl, activate yeast: Mix yeast, sugar, and warm milk. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
    • In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well and add yogurt, melted butter, and yeast mixture.
    • Knead into a soft, smooth dough (add water 1 tbsp at a time if needed). It should be slightly sticky.
    • Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until doubled.
  5. Shape and Cook the Naan:
    • Punch down the dough and divide into 6-8 equal balls.
    • Roll each ball into an oval or teardrop shape (about ¬º inch thick), leaving slightly thicker edges.
    • Sprinkle nigella seeds on top and press gently.
    • Heat a tawa or skillet over high heat. Place naan on it and cook for 1 minute until bubbles form.
    • Flip and cook the other side for 30 seconds. For char, place directly over medium flame using tongs (if gas stove).
    • Brush hot naan with butter. Keep covered in a cloth to stay soft.
    • Repeat for all naans.
  6. Serve:
    • Garnish chole with fresh coriander and onion rings.
    • Serve hot chole with warm naan, lemon wedges, and pickle on the side.
    • Tear naan and scoop up the spicy chole ‚Äî enjoy the perfect bite!
    • Great for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Pairs well with lassi!

Variations & Tips ü•ò

  • Soak chickpeas overnight for best texture; don't skip the tea bag for that authentic dark color.
  • For quicker version, use canned chickpeas but simmer longer for flavor infusion.
  • Brown the onions well ‚Äî it's key to the rich, deep flavor of Punjabi chole.
  • Add a pinch of baking soda while cooking chickpeas if they are hard (speeds up softening).
  • For garlic naan, add minced garlic to the dough or brush on top before cooking.
  • Vegan option: Use plant-based yogurt and oil instead of butter/ghee.
  • Make ahead: Prepare chole a day in advance; flavors deepen overnight.
  • Don't over-knead naan dough ‚Äî it should be soft and pliable.
  • For tandoori-style naan at home, use a cast-iron skillet and finish on open flame.
  • Adjust spice levels: Add more chilies for heat or yogurt to the chole for mildness.
  • Store leftover chole in fridge for 3-4 days; reheat with a splash of water.
  • Freeze naan dough balls for up to a month; thaw and cook fresh.

Vada Pav

Easy

Ingredients

For the Vada (Potato Filling):

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • Juice of half a lemon

For the Batter:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as required to make smooth batter)

For Assembly:

  • 6 pav buns
  • Dry garlic chutney (optional)
  • Green chutney and tamarind chutney
  • Fried green chilies for serving
  • Oil for deep frying

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Potato Filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.
    • When they crackle, add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© for a minute.
    • Add turmeric and salt, then mix in boiled, mashed potatoes.
    • Cook for 2‚Äì3 minutes, then turn off the flame.
    • Add coriander leaves and lemon juice. Mix well and let it cool.
    • Divide into small lemon-sized balls.
  2. Prepare the Batter:
    • In a bowl, mix besan, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt.
    • Add water gradually to form a smooth, thick batter (not runny).
  3. Fry the Vadas:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan.
    • Dip each potato ball into the batter, coat it evenly, and gently drop it into hot oil.
    • Fry on medium flame until golden brown and crisp.
    • Remove and drain on tissue paper.
  4. Assemble the Vada Pav:
    • Slice the pav horizontally without cutting completely.
    • Spread green chutney on one side and tamarind chutney on the other.
    • Sprinkle a bit of dry garlic chutney if desired.
    • Place one hot vada in between and gently press.
    • Serve with fried green chili on the side for that authentic Mumbai touch!

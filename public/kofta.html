Malai Kofta

Royal and indulgent ‚Äî crispy, golden kofta balls made with paneer and potatoes, dunked in a rich, creamy tomato gravy. A showstopper dish for special occasions!

Prep Time

30 mins

Cook Time

35 mins

Serves

4 People

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

For the Kofta:

  • 200g paneer, grated
  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • ¬Ω tsp garam masala
  • ¬Ω tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 10-12 cashews or raisins (for stuffing)
  • Oil for deep frying

For the Gravy:

  • 4 large tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 2 medium onions, roughly chopped
  • 10-12 cashews (for gravy)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 green cardamom pods
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf

Spice Powders:

  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (to balance acidity)

For Finishing:

  • ¬Ω cup fresh cream or malai
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Kofta Mixture:
    • Grate the paneer finely or crumble it well.
    • Boil potatoes, peel, and mash them completely (no lumps!).
    • In a large bowl, mix paneer, mashed potatoes, cornflour, maida, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, coriander, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt.
    • Mix well until everything comes together into a non-sticky dough.
    • If the mixture is too soft, add more cornflour. If too dry, add a little milk.
  2. Shape and Stuff the Koftas:
    • Divide the mixture into 12-14 equal portions.
    • Take one portion, flatten it slightly in your palm.
    • Place a cashew or 2-3 raisins in the center.
    • Seal it well and roll into a smooth, round ball.
    • Make sure there are no cracks (or they'll break while frying).
    • Repeat with all portions and keep aside.
  3. Fry the Koftas:
    • Heat oil in a deep pan on medium-high heat.
    • Test oil by dropping a small piece of mixture ‚Äî it should rise slowly.
    • Gently slide 4-5 koftas into the hot oil.
    • Fry on medium heat, turning gently until golden brown and crispy (5-6 minutes).
    • Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
    • Fry all koftas and set aside. Don't add to gravy until just before serving!
  4. Make the Gravy Base:
    • In a pan, add chopped tomatoes, onions, cashews, green chilies, and 1 cup water.
    • Bring to boil and cook for 10-12 minutes until everything is soft.
    • Let it cool completely, then blend into a smooth puree.
    • Strain the puree for silky smooth gravy (optional but recommended).
  5. Temper the Whole Spices:
    • Heat 3 tbsp oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.
    • Add bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves.
    • Saut√© for 30 seconds until fragrant.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Cook the Gravy:
    • Add the tomato-onion puree to the tempered spices.
    • Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt.
    • Cook on medium heat for 10-12 minutes, stirring frequently.
    • The gravy should thicken and oil should start separating from sides.
    • Add sugar to balance the tanginess.
    • Add water to achieve desired consistency (medium thick).
  7. Finish the Gravy:
    • Lower the heat and add fresh cream, mixing well.
    • Add butter for richness and shine.
    • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add.
    • Sprinkle garam masala and mix.
    • Add saffron milk if using for royal flavor and color.
    • Simmer for 2-3 minutes. The gravy should be smooth and creamy.
  8. Serve:
    • Just before serving, gently place koftas into the hot gravy.
    • Let them soak for 2-3 minutes (don't leave too long or they'll become soggy!).
    • Garnish with fresh cream swirl and coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with naan, kulcha, butter roti, or jeera rice!
    • Perfect for parties, special occasions, or weekend treats!

Variations & Tips ü•ò

  • The key to perfect koftas is the right paneer-to-potato ratio (1:1 or 1:2).
  • Always fry koftas on medium heat for even cooking and golden color.
  • Add koftas to gravy only just before serving to keep them crispy!
  • For extra richness, add khoya (mawa) to the kofta mixture.
  • You can make koftas ahead and store in fridge ‚Äî fry just before serving.
  • For vegan version, use tofu instead of paneer and coconut cream instead of dairy.
  • Strain the gravy for restaurant-style smoothness.
  • Don't skip the cashews in gravy ‚Äî they add creaminess and body.
  • For Lauki Kofta variation, use grated bottle gourd instead of potatoes.
  • For Nargisi Kofta, stuff with boiled eggs instead of nuts.
  • Kasuri methi is essential for authentic dhaba-style flavor.
  • Store extra koftas in freezer for up to a month (fry fresh when needed).

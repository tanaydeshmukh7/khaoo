Solkadi

The refreshing coastal drink from Konkan ‚Äî a beautiful pink digestive made with coconut milk and kokum. Light, tangy, and cooling, it's the perfect accompaniment to spicy seafood and coastal cuisine!

Prep Time

10 mins

Cook Time

5 mins

Serves

4 People

Difficulty

Easy

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 8-10 pieces dried kokum (amsul)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk (fresh or canned)
  • 1 cup water
  • 4-6 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder (optional)

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • A pinch of roasted cumin powder

For Serving:

  • Ice cubes (optional, for chilled version)

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Soak the Kokum:
    • Rinse the dried kokum pieces under water to remove any dirt.
    • Soak them in 1 cup of warm water for 15-20 minutes.
    • Once softened, squeeze the kokum pieces to extract all the juice and color.
    • Strain and keep the pink kokum water aside. Discard the kokum pieces.
  2. Prepare the Coconut Milk:
    • If using fresh coconut, blend grated coconut with water and strain to get thick coconut milk.
    • If using canned coconut milk, shake well before using.
    • Keep the coconut milk at room temperature.
  3. Mix the Solkadi:
    • In a large bowl or jug, add the extracted kokum water.
    • Add the coconut milk and mix well until combined.
    • Add crushed garlic cloves and finely chopped green chilies.
    • Add salt to taste and cumin powder if using.
    • Mix everything together thoroughly.
    • The solkadi should have a beautiful pink color and smooth consistency.
  4. Serve:
    • Pour the solkadi into glasses.
    • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
    • Sprinkle a pinch of roasted cumin powder on top.
    • Serve at room temperature or chilled with ice cubes.
    • Enjoy with spicy fish curry, chicken curry, or any coastal meal!

Tips & Variations ü••

  • Kokum is the star ingredient - it gives solkadi its signature pink color and tangy flavor.
  • Don't boil the solkadi - it's meant to be served raw for maximum cooling effect.
  • Adjust garlic and green chilies according to your preference.
  • For thicker solkadi, use more coconut milk; for lighter version, add more water.
  • Fresh coconut milk gives the best flavor, but canned works great too.
  • Solkadi is a natural digestive and cooling agent - perfect after spicy meals.
  • Some regions add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) for additional flavor.
  • Try adding a small piece of ginger along with garlic for extra zing.
  • Store leftover solkadi in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • Best enjoyed fresh - the flavors are most vibrant when freshly made!

Kachori

The ultimate North Indian street food ‚Äî crispy, flaky pastries stuffed with spiced lentils or peas. Served with tangy tamarind chutney and fluffy aloo sabzi. A breakfast favorite that's absolutely irresistible!

Prep Time

30 mins

Cook Time

25 mins

Serves

6 People

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Water (as needed for kneading)

For the Dal Filling (Moong Dal):

  • 1 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow lentils), soaked for 3-4 hours
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

For Frying:

  • Oil for deep frying

For Serving:

  • Tamarind chutney
  • Green chutney
  • Aloo sabzi (potato curry)

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large bowl, mix flour and salt.
    • Add oil or ghee and rub it into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Gradually add water and knead into a stiff, smooth dough.
    • Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • Drain the soaked moong dal completely and pat dry. Grind it coarsely (not into a paste).
    • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, and asafoetida.
    • When they crackle, add ginger paste and green chilies. Saut√© for 30 seconds.
    • Add the coarsely ground dal and cook on medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring continuously.
    • Add coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala, amchur, and salt.
    • Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the mixture is dry and aromatic.
    • Add chopped coriander leaves, mix, and let the filling cool completely.
  3. Shape the Kachoris:
    • Divide the dough into equal portions (golf ball size).
    • Roll each portion into a small circle, about 3-4 inches in diameter.
    • Place 1-2 tablespoons of filling in the center.
    • Bring the edges together and seal tightly, removing any air pockets.
    • Gently flatten the stuffed ball with your palm.
    • Very carefully roll it into a disc about 4-5 inches in diameter (don't roll too thin or the filling will break through).
    • Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
  4. Fry the Kachoris:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat (not too hot).
    • Gently slide in one kachori at a time.
    • Fry on low to medium flame. Press gently with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
    • Once it puffs up, flip and fry the other side until golden brown.
    • Remove and drain on paper towels.
    • Serve hot with tamarind chutney, green chutney, and aloo sabzi!

Tips & Variations ü•ü

  • The key to puffed kachoris is the right oil temperature - medium heat works best.
  • Make sure the filling is completely dry and not watery, or kachoris won't puff.
  • Don't roll the stuffed kachori too thin, or it will break.
  • Seal the edges properly to prevent filling from coming out during frying.
  • Press gently while frying to help them puff up beautifully.
  • Try matar kachori: Use spiced green peas filling instead of dal.
  • Try pyaaz kachori: Use spiced onion filling for Rajasthani style.
  • Kachoris can be frozen before frying and fried directly from frozen when needed.
  • Best enjoyed fresh and hot with a hot cup of masala chai!

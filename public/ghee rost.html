  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • ¬Ω tsp cumin seeds
  • ¬Ω tsp black peppercorns
  • 4-5 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp water (for grinding)

For Cooking:

  • 6-8 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional, to balance heat)
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh curry leaves
  • Chopped coriander leaves
  • Extra ghee drizzle

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Chicken:
    • Clean and wash the chicken pieces thoroughly.
    • Marinate with turmeric powder, salt, and lemon juice.
    • Set aside for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Make the Spice Paste:
    • Dry roast Kashmiri chilies, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and peppercorns on low heat for 2-3 minutes until aromatic.
    • Let them cool, then grind with garlic, ginger, tamarind paste, and water to make a smooth paste.
    • The paste should be thick and vibrant red.
  3. Cook the Chicken:
    • Heat 3 tbsp ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.
    • Add curry leaves and let them splutter.
    • Add marinated chicken pieces and cook on medium-high heat for 8-10 minutes until they're partially cooked.
    • Remove chicken and set aside.
  4. Saut√© the Onions:
    • In the same pan, add 2 tbsp ghee.
    • Add sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown and caramelized (8-10 minutes).
    • Add slit green chilies and more curry leaves.
  5. Add Spice Paste & Roast:
    • Add the ground spice paste to the onions and cook on low-medium heat for 5-7 minutes.
    • The paste should release oil and turn darker in color.
    • Add remaining ghee generously ‚Äî this is the key to authentic ghee roast!
    • Return the chicken pieces to the pan and coat them well with the masala.
  6. Final Roasting:
    • Cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    • The chicken should be well-coated with the spice mixture and ghee.
    • Add sugar if needed to balance the heat.
    • The dish should have a dry consistency with ghee glistening on top.
  7. Serve:
    • Garnish with fresh curry leaves and coriander.
    • Drizzle extra ghee on top for authentic flavor.
    • Serve hot with neer dosa, appam, rice, or roti.

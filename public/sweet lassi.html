Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh yogurt (curd/dahi) - chilled
  • 1/2 cup cold water or milk
  • 3-4 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

For Garnish:

  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Crushed pistachios or almonds
  • Rose petals (optional)
  • A sprinkle of cardamom powder

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1-2 tsp rose water or kewra water
  • 2-3 drops vanilla essence
  • Fresh cream for extra richness

Step-by-Step Making Process

  1. Prepare the Yogurt:
    • Make sure your yogurt is fresh and chilled.
    • If the yogurt is too thick, it's perfect for lassi!
    • If you have homemade yogurt, that works best.
    • Take the yogurt in a large bowl or blender jar.
  2. Add Sugar and Cardamom:
    • Add sugar to the yogurt.
    • Add cardamom powder for that authentic flavor.
    • If using saffron, soak a few strands in 1 tablespoon of warm milk and add it.
    • If using rose water or kewra water, add it now.
  3. Blend Until Smooth:
    • Add cold water or milk to adjust consistency.
    • Blend everything together using a hand blender or regular blender.
    • Blend for 1-2 minutes until the mixture is smooth, creamy, and frothy.
    • You should see a nice frothy layer on top.
    • If it's too thick, add more water; if too thin, add more yogurt.
  4. Serve Chilled:
    • Add ice cubes to serving glasses.
    • Pour the lassi over the ice.
    • Garnish with crushed pistachios or almonds.
    • Sprinkle a pinch of cardamom powder on top.
    • Add rose petals if using for a beautiful presentation.
    • Serve immediately while it's cold and frothy!

Tips & Variations ü•õ

  • The key to great lassi is using fresh, good-quality yogurt - preferably full-fat.
  • Blend well to achieve that signature frothy top layer.
  • Always serve lassi chilled - it tastes best ice-cold!
  • Don't skip the cardamom - it adds that authentic Punjabi flavor.
  • Adjust sugar according to your preference - some like it very sweet, others prefer mildly sweet.
  • Try mango lassi: Add 1 cup mango pulp or fresh mango chunks while blending.
  • Try strawberry lassi: Add fresh or frozen strawberries while blending.
  • Try malai lassi: Add 2-3 tablespoons of fresh cream for extra richness.
  • Try masala lassi: Skip sugar, add salt, roasted cumin powder, and mint for a savory version.
  • For vegan version: Use coconut yogurt or cashew yogurt with plant-based milk.
  • Lassi aids digestion and is rich in probiotics - perfect after heavy meals!
  • In Punjabi dhabas, lassi is traditionally served in tall steel glasses or earthen kulhads.

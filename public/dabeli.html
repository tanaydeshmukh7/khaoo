Dabeli

The famous Gujarati street food ‚Äî a spicy, sweet, and tangy potato filling stuffed in pav, topped with pomegranate, sev, and peanuts. A flavor explosion from Kutch that's taken India by storm!

Prep Time

15 mins

20 mins

4 People

Difficulty

Easy

Ingredients

For the Dabeli Masala:

  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 dry red chilies
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 4-5 black peppercorns
  • (Or use 3 tbsp readymade dabeli masala)

For the Filling:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 3 tbsp dabeli masala (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2 tbsp tamarind chutney
  • 1 tbsp jaggery or sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

For Assembly:

  • 8 pav buns
  • Butter for toasting
  • Garlic chutney
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Green chutney
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1 cup fine sev (nylon sev)
  • Finely chopped onions (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare Dabeli Masala (Optional - if making from scratch):
    • Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, cloves, cinnamon, red chilies, fennel seeds, and peppercorns.
    • Roast on low heat until aromatic (about 2-3 minutes).
    • Let it cool completely, then grind to a fine powder.
    • Store in an airtight container or use immediately.
  2. Prepare the Potato Filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan.
    • Add dabeli masala and saut√© for 30 seconds on low heat.
    • Add tamarind chutney, jaggery, and salt. Mix well.
    • Add water and let it come to a boil.
    • Add mashed potatoes and mix everything together.
    • Cook for 3-4 minutes until the mixture comes together and is slightly dry.
    • Add chopped coriander leaves and mix.
    • Turn off heat and let the filling cool.
  3. Toast the Pav:
    • Slice the pav horizontally without cutting all the way through.
    • Spread garlic chutney on both inner sides of the pav.
    • Heat a griddle or tawa and add a little butter.
    • Toast the pav on both sides until golden and crispy.
    • Remove and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Dabeli:
    • Open the toasted pav and spread green chutney on one side.
    • Spread tamarind chutney on the other side.
    • Place a generous portion of the potato filling in the center.
    • Top with chopped onions (if using).
    • Sprinkle crushed roasted peanuts generously.
    • Add pomegranate seeds for that burst of freshness.
    • Top with a generous amount of fine sev.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Gently press the dabeli and serve immediately while it's crispy!

Tips & Variations ü•ú

  • The key to great dabeli is the balance of sweet, spicy, and tangy flavors.
  • Adjust jaggery and tamarind according to your taste preference.
  • Toast the pav with butter for that authentic street food taste.
  • Don't skip the pomegranate - it adds a wonderful burst of freshness!
  • Use fresh sev for the best crunch - add it just before serving.
  • The filling can be made ahead and refrigerated for 2-3 days.
  • Try cheese dabeli: Add grated cheese along with other toppings.
  • Try schezwan dabeli: Mix some schezwan sauce in the filling for a fusion twist.
  • For spicier dabeli, add more red chili powder to the filling.
  • Serve with fried green chilies and additional chutneys on the side!

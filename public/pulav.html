Vegetable Pulav

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 1¬Ω cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 3 cups water
  • Salt to taste

For the Vegetables:

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, corn)
  • 1 medium potato, cubed
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

Whole Spices:

  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 black cardamom (optional)
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2-3 black peppercorns
  • 1 star anise (optional)

Ground Spices:

  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Other Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves
  • 2 tbsp fried onions (for garnish)
  • A few drops of lemon juice

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse basmati rice 2-3 times until water runs clear.
    • Soak in water for 30 minutes, then drain completely.
    • This helps in getting perfectly separated, fluffy rice grains.
  2. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Wash and chop all vegetables into uniform small pieces.
    • Keep carrots, beans, peas, corn, and potatoes ready.
    • Finely chop onion, tomato, and green chilies.
    • Chop mint and coriander leaves for garnishing.
  3. Temper the Whole Spices:
    • Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, peppercorns, and star anise.
    • Saut√© for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Saut√© Aromatics and Vegetables:
    • Add sliced onions and green chilies, saut√© until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute until raw smell disappears.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
    • Add all the mixed vegetables and potato cubes.
    • Stir well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add Spices and Rice:
    • Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
    • Mix well and cook for 1 minute to roast the spices.
    • Add the drained rice and gently mix with the vegetables.
    • Saut√© for 2 minutes, coating rice with spices (be gentle to avoid breaking grains).
  6. Cook the Pulav (Choose Your Method):
    • Stovetop Method: Add 3 cups water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover with a tight lid, and cook for 15-18 minutes until rice is done. Turn off heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.
    • Pressure Cooker Method: Add 3 cups water, close the lid, and cook for 1 whistle on high heat. Turn off the heat and let pressure release naturally.
    • Rice Cooker Method: Transfer everything to a rice cooker, add water, and cook on rice setting.
  7. Final Touch:
    • Once cooked, gently fluff the pulav with a fork.
    • Sprinkle garam masala and mix gently.
    • Add chopped mint and coriander leaves.
    • Drizzle a few drops of lemon juice for freshness.
  8. Serve:
    • Transfer pulav to a serving dish.
    • Garnish with fried onions and fresh herbs.
    • Serve hot with raita, papad, pickle, and a squeeze of lemon!
    • Perfect for lunch boxes, dinner parties, or a comforting weeknight meal.

Variations & Tips üåø

  • Always use aged basmati rice for the best texture and aroma.
  • The rice-to-water ratio is crucial: 1:2 for perfect fluffy pulav.
  • Don't skip soaking the rice ‚Äî it reduces cooking time and prevents mushiness.
  • You can add cashews and raisins for a richer, festive version.
  • For extra flavor, use vegetable stock instead of plain water.
  • Add a pinch of saffron soaked in warm milk for special occasions.
  • Paneer cubes or boiled eggs can be added for extra protein.
  • Make it spicier by adding more green chilies or red chili powder.
  • Leftover pulav can be transformed into pulav cutlets or fried rice!
  • Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days.
